Cultural Traditions

Thanksgiving is a holiday celebrated in Canada to give thanks to a good agricultural year as well as a good giving thanks to the gifts and blessings received throughout the year. Canada celebrates Thanksgiving just like its neighboring country America. Unlike the US; however, Canada celebrates its Thanksgiving on Monday the second week of October. ( For example the holiday will be celebrated on October 10th in the year 2022. ) In 1879, Canada finally acknowledged Thanksgiving as a national holiday. The holiday; however, did not always take place on the Monday of the second week of October. From 1879 to 1907 the holiday took place on November 6th; but in 1908 the holiday was moved to the following Monday in order to extend the week preceding it. On Thanksgiving it is tradition as well to have a feast as part of the celebration of the holiday. As stated by CountryLiving, “Traditionally, this included roast turkey and seasonal produce, such as pumpkin, corn ears and pecan nuts.”

About Canadian Culture

Canada’s culture is not as distinct from their neighboring country America then what appears at first glance. Canada’s culture has been described as a mix between the countries of British, French, and American culture. Canada is often described as being somewhat in line with America's culture, thus being a collective of other countries' cultural aspects combined to make a new unique culture. As stated by Britannica, “In 1951 the Royal Commission on National Development in the Arts, Letters, and Sciences issued a report (what became known as the Massey Report) warning that Canadian culture had become invisible,”
